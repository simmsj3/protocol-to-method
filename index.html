From Protocol to Methods: Spider Plant Experiment

A Guide for Biochemistry Students

Converting Your Spider Plant and Jiffy Pellet Experiment Protocol into a Scientific Methods Section

1. Use Third-Person Past Tense

What is Third-Person Past Tense?

Definition: Third-person past tense refers to a writing style that describes actions or events that have already occurred, using pronouns such as "it," "he," "she," "they," or the names of people or things, rather than "I" or "we."

Guidance:

  • Use "was" or "were" instead of "is" or "are"
  • Avoid personal pronouns like "I," "we," or "you"
  • Focus on what happened, not who did it
  • Use passive voice when the doer of the action is not important
  • Examples:
  • "The experiment was conducted..." (not "We conducted...")
  • "Spider plant cuttings were planted..." (not "Plant the cuttings...")
  • "Data were analyzed..." (not "Analyze the data...")

Quiz: Question 1

Which sentence is correct for a methods section?

A) We watered the plants daily.

B) The plants were watered daily.

C) Water the plants daily.

D) Plants are watered daily.

E) I ensured the plants were watered daily.

Correct answer: B) The plants were watered daily.

2. Materials and Equipment

  • List all necessary items, chemicals, and instruments
  • Use passive voice
  • Example: "A 250mL beaker was used for solution preparation."
  • Example: "Chlorophytum comosum (spider plant) cuttings and Jiffy-7 peat pellets were utilized in this experiment."

3. Experimental Setup

  • Explain how equipment was arranged or prepared
  • Describe the overall experimental design
  • Example: "The experiment was set up in a controlled environment chamber. Jiffy pellets were arranged in trays under LED grow lights."

4. Procedure

  • Convert protocol steps into paragraphs
  • Group related steps together
  • Include any controls or repetitions
  • Example: "Spider plant cuttings were inserted into pre-moistened Jiffy pellets. The trays were placed under LED grow lights set to a 16/8 hour light/dark cycle. Plants were watered daily with 50mL of distilled water per pellet. This procedure was repeated for three separate batches to ensure reproducibility."

5. Data Collection Methods

  • Describe what data was recorded and how
  • Include frequency and methods of measurement
  • Example: "Plant height was measured weekly using a ruler. The number of new leaves was counted every three days. Root emergence was monitored daily and recorded as present or absent."

6. Data Analysis Techniques

  • Mention statistical tests or calculations performed
  • Include software used for analysis
  • Example: "Growth rates were calculated using linear regression. A one-way ANOVA was performed to compare growth rates between different light intensities. All statistical analyses were conducted using R (version 4.1.2)."

7. Safety and Ethical Considerations

  • Note any safety precautions taken
  • Mention any ethical approvals obtained
  • Example: "All plant waste was autoclaved before disposal to prevent environmental contamination. The experimental protocol was approved by the institutional biosafety committee (approval number: BSC-2023-045)."

Final Tips

  • Ensure all steps are written in third-person past tense
  • Provide enough detail for experiment replication
  • Maintain a logical flow throughout the methods section
  • Proofread carefully to eliminate errors and improve clarity
